Both keto and carnivore are low-carb approaches that trigger fat burning — but they work differently, suit different people, and produce different results. If you're trying to decide between the two, here's everything you need to know.

## What Is the Keto Diet?

The ketogenic diet is a **high-fat, moderate-protein, very low-carb** diet. The goal is to get your body into a metabolic state called **ketosis**, where it burns fat for fuel instead of glucose.

**Typical keto macros:**
- Fat: 65–75% of calories
- Protein: 20–30% of calories
- Carbs: 5–10% of calories (under 20–50g net carbs/day)

**Foods allowed on keto:**
- Meat, fish, eggs
- Cheese, butter, heavy cream
- Avocados, olive oil, nuts
- Low-carb vegetables (leafy greens, broccoli, zucchini)
- Berries (in moderation)

## What Is the Carnivore Diet?

The carnivore diet takes things further — it's a **zero-carb** diet consisting exclusively of animal products.

**Foods allowed on carnivore:**
- Beef, pork, lamb, poultry, organ meats
- Fish and seafood
- Eggs
- Some dairy (butter, heavy cream, hard cheese — optional)
- Salt, water

**No vegetables, no fruits, no plant foods of any kind.**

## Key Differences

| | Keto | Carnivore |
|---|---|---|
| Carbs | Under 20–50g/day | Zero (0g) |
| Plant foods | Yes (low-carb) | No |
| Simplicity | Moderate | Very simple |
| Food variety | High | Low |
| Gut inflammation | Some improvement | Maximum reduction |
| Social ease | Manageable | Difficult |
| Cost | Moderate | Higher |

## Which Is Better for Weight Loss?

Both diets produce significant weight loss — often more than traditional low-fat diets. However:

- **Keto** works well for most people and is sustainable long-term
- **Carnivore** often produces faster initial weight loss due to complete elimination of all plant compounds and the satiety of protein + fat

If you've tried keto and stalled, carnivore can often break the plateau.

## Which Is Better for Inflammation?

Carnivore wins here. Many people report dramatic reductions in:
- Joint pain and arthritis
- Skin conditions (acne, eczema, psoriasis)
- Digestive issues (IBS, bloating)
- Autoimmune symptoms

This is because it eliminates all plant antinutrients (lectins, oxalates, phytates) that can trigger inflammation in sensitive people.

## Who Should Do Keto?

Keto is right for you if:
- You're new to low-carb eating
- You enjoy food variety
- You want flexibility in social situations
- Your goals are weight loss and general health improvement
- You do well with vegetables

## Who Should Do Carnivore?

Carnivore is right for you if:
- You have autoimmune conditions or chronic inflammation
- You've hit a weight loss plateau on keto
- You want maximum simplicity (no tracking, no counting)
- You struggle with food addiction or cravings
- You want to eliminate all food variables to identify sensitivities

## Can You Combine Both?

Yes — many people do **"keto carnivore"**: eating mostly meat and animal products but allowing some low-carb vegetables and dairy. This is a practical middle ground that captures most of the benefits of both approaches.

## The Bottom Line

There's no universal winner. Keto is the better starting point for most people — it's flexible, sustainable, and well-researched. Carnivore is a powerful tool for those dealing with inflammation, stalls, or seeking maximum simplicity.
